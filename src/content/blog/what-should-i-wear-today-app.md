Every morning. The same ceiling. The same question.

"What am I actually going to wear today?"

You have clothes. Lots of them. And somehow the answer is never obvious. You stand there, five minutes ticking by, and eventually grab the same three things you always grab — the ones that require zero thinking.

Spoiler: they didn't get easier. They got worse.

## The Closet Full of Nothing

There's a name for this feeling in behavioral research: **decision fatigue paired with availability bias**. Your brain can only hold so many options in working memory at once. In the morning, when your cognitive resources are lowest, the number is even smaller.

So you reach for the familiar. The black jeans. The go-to blazer. The shoes you wear four days a week. Not because they're the best options — but because they're the ones your tired brain can surface.

The other stuff might as well not exist.

I had a cream linen blazer I legitimately forgot I owned for six months. Six months. It was right there in my closet the whole time. I "discovered" it again while looking for something else and immediately wore it three times that week.

That's the problem. Not the clothes. The visibility.
