I used to think I had a "versatile wardrobe." Three blazers. Seven dresses. Twenty tops. Options for every occasion.

Then I actually tracked what I wore for 30 days straight.

The data didn't lie. I wore the same navy blazer 18 times. The black ankle boots — 22 times. The white ribbed tank? 15 times. My "versatile" wardrobe was 8 pieces in heavy rotation and 95 pieces collecting dust.

---

## The Experiment

It started as a bet with myself. Could I log every outfit for a month without it becoming a second job?

Rules were simple:
- Photo or text log every morning (30 seconds max)
- Note: occasion, weather, comfort 1–10, "would wear again tomorrow" yes/no
- No editing. No curating. Even the "I gave up and wore leggings" days.

I used a notes app. No fancy tags. Just:
```
7/24 — Client pitch, 78°F, sunny
Navy blazer, white tank, straight-leg jeans, black boots
Comfort: 9. Wear again: yes.
```

---

## What the Numbers Showed

**Top 8 pieces = 67% of all outfits.**

| Piece | Wears | Occasions Covered |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| Navy blazer | 18 | Work, dinner, travel, school pickup |
| Black boots | 22 | Everything except beach |
| White ribbed tank | 15 | Base layer for 4 formulas |
| Straight-leg jeans | 12 | Casual Friday, weekend, travel |
| Camel coat | 9 | Commute, client dinner, airport |
| Black loafers | 11 | Office, errands, dinner |
| Grey cardigan | 8 | WFH, layering, plane |
| Black midi dress | 6 | Date night, wedding guest, presentation |

The other 95 pieces? 31 wore **zero times**. 42 wore 1–2 times (mostly "special occasion" items for occasions that never came). 22 wore 3–5 times (the "I like this but..." pieces — wrong sleeve length, slightly tight, annoying fabric).

---

## The Formula Extraction

By week two, patterns snapped into focus. I wasn't choosing outfits. I was assembling formulas.

**Formula 1: The Daily Driver**  
White tank + straight jeans + navy blazer + black boots  
*Work, coffee run, school pickup, client call — same bones, swap shoes for occasion.*

**Formula 2: The One-Piece Wonder**  
Black midi dress + camel coat + black boots  
*Date night, wedding, presentation — add blazer or jewelry to dial formality.*

**Formula 3: The WFH Uniform**  
Grey cardigan + white tank + leggings + house shoes  
*Comfort 10/10. Camera-ready waist up. Zero decision energy.*

**Formula 4: The Travel Stack**  
White tank + jeans + cardigan + blazer + boots (worn on plane)  
*Every layer removable. Works 55°F to 80°F. Fits in carry-on.*

Four formulas. Thirty days. Zero "I have nothing to wear" mornings.

---

## The Ghosts in My Closet

The 31 never-worn pieces told their own story:

- **3 silk blouses** — dry clean only, wrinkle sitting down, zero stretch
- **2 printed midi skirts** — cute on hanger, no top matches, waist gap
- **1 sequin top** — bought for "holiday parties" (attended 0 in 3 years)
- **4 trendy cuts** — asymmetrical hem, cold shoulder, cutout back, puff sleeve — all uncomfortable sitting
- **The "aspirational size" jeans** — kept for motivation, served only guilt

These weren't style failures. They were **context failures**. I bought them for a life I don't live (events I don't attend, weather I don't have, comfort I won't tolerate).

---

## The Friday Review That Changed Everything

The protocol says: every Friday, 5 minutes. Mark each outfit ✅ loved, 🔄 tweak, ❌ skip.

Week 1 Friday: 3 ✅, 4 🔄, 0 ❌  
Week 4 Friday: 6 ✅, 1 🔄, 0 ❌

The 🔄 items became my shopping list — but surgical. Not "need a new top." Specifically: "need a second navy blazer in lighter wool for summer" and "need black loafers with rubber sole for rainy days."

I bought two things in month two. Both filled exact formula gaps. Both hit 10+ wears in month three.

---

## What This Means for You

You don't need 100 pieces. You need 8–12 that *actually work together* across your real occasions.

The closet audit question isn't "Do I love this?" It's **"Did I wear this last month? Will I wear it next month?"**

If the answer is no, it's not wardrobe. It's storage.
